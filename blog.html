Climate-Smart Farming in Kenya: Why It Matters

With erratic rainfall patterns and prolonged droughts in counties like Kitui and Machakos, climate-smart agriculture is no longer optional—it's essential. At Kioko Farms, we promote drip irrigation, mulching, and drought-resistant crops to ensure food security and sustainability in arid and semi-arid lands (ASALs).

Why More Kenyan Youth Should Embrace Agribusiness

Agriculture is not just for the old generation. With access to technology, social media marketing, and mobile money platforms like M-PESA, the youth in Kenya can modernize agribusiness. Kioko Farms runs mentorship sessions to guide young farmers in starting agribusiness ventures—from greenhouse farming to poultry.

Urban Farming: Growing Sukuma and Spinach in Nairobi Flats

Don’t have land? No problem. Urban farming using vertical sacks, containers, or balconies is becoming popular in cities like Nairobi, Kisumu, and Mombasa. We show you how to grow sukuma wiki, spinach, and even tomatoes from the comfort of your apartment block. Perfect for reducing market costs and promoting healthy eating!

How to Start a Profitable Chicken Project in Kenya

Whether you're in Bungoma or Embu, poultry farming can be a reliable income stream. Start small with 50 layers or broilers, ensure quality feed, clean housing, and follow vaccination schedules. Kioko Farms can help with starter kits, feed planning, and market access via online platforms.
