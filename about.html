About Kioko Farms

Rooted in tradition. Growing with purpose.

Empowering Learners Through CBC Integration

Kioko Farms actively embraces Kenya’s Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC) to offer practical, hands-on learning experiences. We partner with schools to turn our farm into a real-life classroom where learners engage in experiential learning tied to core CBC competencies such as environmental conservation, critical thinking, problem-solving, and creativity.

Our CBC-aligned programs include guided tours, farm-based science projects, agriculture-themed assignments, and interactive farm diaries. We allow learners to plant, nurture, harvest, and document agricultural cycles—fostering ownership and a deep understanding of food systems. Students learn not just to grow crops, but also how to calculate yields, assess soil quality, and apply eco-friendly solutions, bridging theory with real-world application.

Youth in Agriculture: Cultivating the Future

At Kioko Farms, we view the youth as the drivers of tomorrow's food systems. Through our Youth in Agriculture Initiative, we train, mentor, and empower young people to embrace agribusiness as a modern, profitable, and innovative career path. We debunk myths that agriculture is old-fashioned or unskilled work by introducing youth to mechanized tools, climate-smart practices, agritech, and entrepreneurial thinking.

Programs include:

  • Fellowships and internships for university students
  • Weekend farming clubs for high school youth
  • Access to seed funding for youth-led garden projects
  • Training in digital marketing for farm products

Through exposure, mentorship, and real investment in skills, we’re building a new generation of food leaders, innovators, and agri-entrepreneurs.

Kitchen Garden Setups: Growing More with Less

Food security starts at home. That’s why Kioko Farms trains households, schools, and youth groups on how to establish productive kitchen gardens—even in tight urban spaces. These small-scale gardens make use of creative structures such as sack gardens, hanging containers, vertical towers, and recycled plastic buckets.

Our kitchen garden packages include:

  • Customized garden layout based on your space
  • Free seedlings and organic manure
  • Workshops on pest control and composting
  • Monthly check-ins and harvest support

From kale and spinach to tomatoes, carrots, and herbs, families can reduce their grocery expenses while improving their nutrition and gaining farming skills.

Life on the Farm: A Community in Motion

Kioko Farms isn’t just a place of work—it’s a lifestyle, a community, and a story of harmony between people and nature. A typical day begins with early morning irrigation, followed by livestock care, training sessions, school visits, and group workshops. Volunteers help collect eggs, prune vegetables, and document growth patterns for research.

Life here is punctuated by stories under the mango trees, laughter in the greenhouse, and evening bonfires where experiences are shared. We balance hard work with mentorship, education, and joy—creating a space where people reconnect with the land and each other. Our commitment to sustainability is evident in every action—from water harvesting and organic composting to energy-efficient tools and ecological preservation zones.
